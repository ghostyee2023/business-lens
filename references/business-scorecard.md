# Business Scorecard

Score 1-5. Add one short reason for each score.

## Demand

- Pain frequency: repeated daily/weekly beats rare.
- Pain intensity: revenue, risk, reputation, time, or emotional cost.
- Urgency: whether delay hurts.
- Current workaround cost: money, time, error, coordination, or lost opportunity.

## Buyer

- Buyer clarity: named payer or budget owner.
- Budget fit: existing spend category or willingness to create one.
- Switching reason: why now, why this, why not current solution.
- Trust barrier: how much proof is required before purchase.

## Distribution

- First 10 users: whether they can be reached directly.
- Channel fit: audience, community, search, marketplace, partnership, existing client base.
- Sales complexity: self-serve, assisted, founder-led, enterprise.

## Delivery

- MVP deliverability: whether a small version can create value quickly.
- Gross effort: time and human labor needed per customer.
- Repeatability: whether delivery gets easier after each customer.
- Support burden: whether usage creates too much handholding.

## Defensibility

- Workflow depth: whether it embeds into repeated work.
- Data or template compounding: whether outputs improve the asset.
- Switching cost: whether users build history, templates, or habits.
- Differentiation: why it is not a generic AI wrapper.

## Decision Rule

```text
Proceed: demand + buyer + validation path are strong.
Reshape: demand is real but buyer/model/channel is weak.
Park: interesting but access or timing is weak.
Reject: no clear repeated pain, buyer, or value capture.
```

Do not average scores blindly. A zero-buyer idea is not rescued by a beautiful product.
