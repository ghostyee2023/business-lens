# Commercial Logic Check

Use this to judge whether an opportunity can plausibly become a small business or productized offer.

## Logic Chain

Check the full chain:

```text
user
-> painful job
-> current workaround
-> better outcome
-> buyer
-> payment reason
-> acquisition path
-> delivery mechanism
-> repeatability
-> defensibility or compounding asset
```

If one link is missing, the idea may still be useful, but the business logic is incomplete.

## Core Questions

- Who feels the pain?
- Who pays?
- Is the payer the same person as the user?
- What budget or spending category does this replace?
- What do they do today instead?
- Why would they switch now?
- How often does the pain happen?
- What is the cost of doing nothing?
- Can the product deliver value before large integrations or a big dataset?
- How will the first 10 users be reached?

## Business Logic Smells

- User loves it, but no one owns the budget.
- It saves time, but the saved time is not valuable to the buyer.
- It requires behavior change before value appears.
- It depends on a platform that can copy or block the product.
- It is useful only once.
- It is a feature, not a workflow.
- It needs trust before it can prove value.
- It competes with a good-enough free workaround.

## Strong Signals

- The user already pays for a worse workaround.
- The pain creates revenue loss, risk, delay, embarrassment, or churn.
- The buyer has a clear budget line.
- The first version can be delivered manually.
- The output is used in a repeated workflow.
- Distribution is attached to an existing audience, community, channel, or client base.
